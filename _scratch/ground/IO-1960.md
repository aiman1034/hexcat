VERIFICATION / FLAGS:
- **SwK & Durchsatz are verbatim, NOT port-math.** Note the WebSearch snippet was garbled (claimed JL805A=131 Mpps and "throughput=switching capacity" duplicates) — those are WRONG. OEM table: JL805A **238 Mpps / 320 Gbps**; JL806A & JL807A **95 Mpps / 128 Gbps**; JL808A & JL809A **131 Mpps / 176 Gbps**.
- **Port totals**: JL805A = 12 access (10GBASE-T) + 4 SFP+ = 16. The 24-/48-port models each add 2 SFP+ **and** 2 10GBASE-T = +4 uplinks (28 / 52 total). Uplink block is 2×SFP+ **plus** 2×10GBASE-T — both must be encoded, don't collapse to "2 uplinks".
- **JL806A is the fan-less one** (only 24-port non-PoE). All others have variable-speed fans.
- **Stacking = 4 units max** (True Stacking, cloud-managed) — this is managed L2+ stackable, consistent with the manifest classification.
- Datasheet has a typo cluster on p.12 ("IEEE 802.2af/at/bt" — should be 802.3af/at/bt); the correct 802.3af/at/bt classes appear in the port-config and PoE-table body, use those.
- No B-revs or regional/TAA variants exist for 1960 (unlike the 1930 JL683B/684B/686B lane). Roster is a clean 5.
